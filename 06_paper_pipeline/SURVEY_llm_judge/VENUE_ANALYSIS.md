## TMLR — Transactions on Machine Learning Research (Primary)

### Why first
- **Free**: $0 APC, $0 author fee, $0 publication fee.
- **Fast for a journal**: 3–6 month review cycle is competitive with conferences.
- **Indexed**: DBLP, Google Scholar, OpenReview, growing presence on Semantic Scholar.
- **Modern review model**: action-editor + 3 reviewers; open review (post-acceptance).
- **Survey Certification**: explicit track for comprehensive surveys.
- **No author-status gate**: TMLR explicitly states reviews evaluate the work, not the authors. Solo first-time authors are not penalised.
- **Reputation rising**: HELM (Liang et al. 2022) at TMLR has ~1,500 citations. TMLR is now considered top-tier ML by many committees.

### Honest weaknesses
- Newer than JMLR; some hiring committees and immigration reviewers still less familiar with it.
- Acceptance rate for surveys not publicly tracked.
- Open review means the rejection (if any) is publicly visible.

### Submission process
1. Format paper in TMLR LaTeX template (style file from openreview.net).
2. Create OpenReview account.
3. Submit via OpenReview.
4. Action editor assigned within ~2 weeks.
5. 3 reviewers invited; reviews within 6–10 weeks of assignment.
6. Author response window (2 weeks).
7. Decision: Accept / Accept with minor revisions / Reject with possibility to resubmit / Reject.
8. If accepted: camera-ready (~2 weeks), publication (~1 week after camera-ready).

### Survey Certification specifics
- Comprehensive surveys earn a "Survey Certification" badge.
- Discussion at https://www.jmlr.org/tmlr/certifications.html
- Must demonstrate: comprehensiveness, structured analysis, identified open problems.

### Submission timing
- TMLR is rolling — submit when ready.
- Recommended: month 10 of execution (Feb–Mar 2027).
- Decision expected: month 13–14 (May–Jun 2027).
- Publication: month 14–15 (Jun–Jul 2027).

### USCIS notes
- TMLR is peer-reviewed and indexed → counts for NIW Prong 2 (well-positioned) and EB-1A criterion (vi).
- Open-review pages are public → easier to verify in petition exhibits.
- "Survey Certified" badge can be cited in petition as third-party recognition.

## ACM Computing Surveys (CSUR) — Fallback 1

### Why second
- **Free with Green OA option**: author retains right to post on personal website / arXiv.
- **Very high impact factor**: ~14 (one of the highest in CS).
- **USCIS gold standard**: ACM journals are universally recognised.
- **Long-form**: 50–80 page surveys are the norm.

### Honest weaknesses
- **Slow**: 8–18 months from submission to decision.
- **Selective**: comprehensive review standard; some surveys are desk-rejected for scope.
- **Editorial gate**: a senior editor pre-screens; solo first-time authors face higher bar.
- **Subscription paywall**: non-OA articles are behind paywall (Green OA mitigates).

### Submission process
1. Format in ACM template.
2. Submit via Manuscript Central (ACM portal).
3. EIC assigns to area editor (1–4 weeks).
4. 3 reviewers; reviews 3–6 months.
5. Major-revision cycle typical (sometimes 2 rounds).
6. Decision within 8–18 months total.

### Submission timing
- If TMLR rejects: submit to ACM CS at month 14–15.
- ACM CS decision: month 22–32.
- Publication: month 24–34.

### USCIS notes
- ACM CS is among the strongest free venues for EB-1A criterion (vi).
- Impact factor of ~14 is a quantifiable strength in petitions.

## JMLR — Journal of Machine Learning Research — Fallback 2

### Why third
- **Free**: longest-running free top-tier ML journal.
- **Maximum prestige**: cited in tenure cases, ICML/NeurIPS award committees.
- **Surveys here become canonical**: JMLR surveys often hit 1,000+ citations.

### Honest weaknesses
- **Very slow**: 12–24 months not unusual.
- **Highly selective for surveys**: explicit author authority expectation in practice.
- **Solo first-author surveys are rare**: feasible but uphill.

### Submission process
1. Format in JMLR LaTeX template.
2. Submit via JMLR submission system.
3. Action editor assignment (2–6 weeks).
4. Reviews: 6–18 months.
5. Multiple revision rounds standard.

### Submission timing
- If TMLR and CSUR both reject: submit to JMLR at month 25–30.
- Total elapsed time before publication: potentially 36–48 months.

### USCIS notes
- JMLR publication is **the** strongest free-venue exhibit for EB-1A (vi).
- A JMLR survey alone meets or exceeds the standard for "original contribution of major significance" (criterion v).

## Computational Linguistics journal (MIT Press) — Fallback 3

### Why fourth
- **Free**: open-access NLP journal of record (since 2019).
- **NLP-specific authority**: read by the LLM-judging community directly.
- **Peer-reviewed**: rigorous, traditional journal review.

### Honest weaknesses
- Less prestigious than JMLR / ACM CS in pure-ML circles.
- Narrower readership (NLP specifically vs broader ML).
- Survey acceptance rates not transparent.

### USCIS notes
- Recognised peer-reviewed journal → counts for NIW and EB-1A.
- Less impact factor weight than TMLR / ACM CS / JMLR in general AI/ML contexts.

## Why we are NOT considering these venues

### Frontiers in AI (~$2,500 APC)
- Violates the free-venue constraint.
- Quality-mixed reputation in ML.

### PLOS ONE / PLOS Computational Biology
- APC applies.
- Not the right scope.

### IEEE Access ($1,995 APC)
- Violates the free-venue constraint.
- Lower prestige for surveys.

### MDPI journals (various)
- APC applies.
- Universally negative perception in ML hiring committees.

### "International Journal of ..." with APC
- Default-suspicious.
- Likely predatory.

### NeurIPS / ICML / ICLR main tracks
- Conferences accept surveys only as workshops typically.
- Length limit (~9 pages) too short for comprehensive survey.
- Reserve these for empirical papers (T02, T07).

### EMNLP / ACL Findings
- 8-page limit too short.
- Could host a "short survey" version after the main one is published, but not the primary venue.

## Strategic submission plan

```
Month 10 (Feb 2027): Submit to TMLR Survey Certification track.
                     Post arXiv preprint same day.
                     Activate marketing plan (Twitter, GitHub repo, newsletters).

Month 13–14 (May 2027): TMLR decision.
                         Case A: Accept → camera-ready → publication month 15.
                         Case B: Reject → revise and resubmit to TMLR (one cycle), OR
                                 escalate to ACM Computing Surveys.

If TMLR rejects with no resubmit option:
Month 15 (Jul 2027): Submit revised version to ACM Computing Surveys.
Month 22–32: Decision.
              Accept → publication.
              Reject → escalate to JMLR.

If ACM CS rejects:
Month 25–30 (Jun–Nov 2028): Submit to JMLR.
                              Long review cycle; ultimate fallback.

Throughout all cycles: arXiv preprint accumulates citations regardless of journal acceptance.
                       This is critical — even if all journals reject, the arXiv version of a
                       high-quality comprehensive survey reaches 100+ citations purely on merit.
```

## Backup-of-the-backup: arXiv-only "in-progress" survey

If for any reason no journal accepts within 24 months:
- Keep the survey on arXiv with version updates (v1, v2, ...).
- Update annually with new sections / new papers covered.
- Maintain the companion GitHub repo as the de facto living version.
- arXiv-only surveys can still hit 500+ citations (Bommasani et al. 2021 is arXiv-only and has 8,000+ citations).

For USCIS purposes: arXiv-only would be the weakest evidence type, but combined
with T02 + T07 peer-reviewed papers, the overall portfolio still meets NIW / EB-1A standards.

## Final recommendation

**Submit to TMLR Survey Certification at month 10.**

Rationale: highest probability of acceptance × fastest decision × strong indexing × no APC × no author-status gate × growing prestige.

Fallback: ACM Computing Surveys → JMLR → Computational Linguistics → arXiv-only.

No venue in the fallback chain costs anything.
